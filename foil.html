What Is Foil?

Foil is one of the three modern Olympic fencing weapons. It is a light, flexible thrusting weapon designed to emphasize discipline, precision, and technique. Historically used as a training weapon for dueling, foil fencing emphasizes form and carefully structured attacks.

Only the torso is a valid target, simulating the vital areas of the body. Because foil uses the right-of-way rule, fencers must follow strict tactical conventions to earn the priority needed to score.

Rules of Foil

  • Right-of-Way: Priority rules determine who is awarded the touch if both fencers hit. Attacks, parries, and ripostes follow a clear order.
  • Target Area: Only the torso (front and back) counts as valid target, excluding arms, legs, and head.
  • Scoring: Points are scored with the tip of the foil on the valid target area.
  • Bout Format: Standard bouts last three 3-minute periods or end when one fencer reaches 15 touches.
  • Equipment: Fencers wear a metallic lamé jacket to register valid touches electronically.
  • Penalties: Offenses include turning one’s back, covering target area, or failing to follow right-of-way conventions.
